Infection Control Guidelines

Hover or tap the cards below to reveal the foundational standards.

Australian Guidelines for the Prevention and Control of Infection in Healthcare

(AICG/NHMRC 2019)

The foundational document providing a nationally accepted approach to infection prevention. It is underpinned by a risk-management framework that guides clinicians in identifying and mitigating biological hazards.

AS 5369:2023

National Standard

The uniform national standard for the reprocessing of reusable medical devices (RMDs) and other devices. This standard governs everything from quality systems and staff competency to the specific requirements for validated reprocessing procedures.

AS/NZS 5369:2023

Amd 1:2026 [Current]

The most recent amendment to the national standard. It provides updated requirements for reprocessing in both health and non-health related facilities, ensuring that the latest technological and clinical safety advancements are integrated into daily practice.

ADA Infection Control Guidelines

(Current Edition)

A practical resource specifically designed by the Australian Dental Association to synthesise national requirements. It translates complex standards into actionable protocols tailored for the unique challenges of the dental environment.
